### Identified 3 Data Quality Risks

During the development of the Medallion Architecture (Bronze-Silver-Gold) ETL pipeline, three critical technical risks were identified and mitigated to ensure the integrity of the final analytical results:

1. Handling inconsistent CSV structures

The raw data wasn't uniform, older files had 17 columns, while newer ones included an 18th (congestion_surcharge). A standard COPY command would simply crash on such a mismatch.

Fix: I designed the Bronze layer to be schema-agnostic, mapping missing columns to NULL. The actual structure is enforced only later in the Silver layer through explicit casting, ensuring the pipeline never breaks during ingestion.

2. Filtering corrupted records
The raw dataset was "dirty". I found trips dated in the year 2090 and records with negative fares or negative distances. Leaving these in would have rendered the final profit analysis useless.

Fix: I implemented a set of Sanity Checks during the transition to Silver. This logic automatically drops any records that violate physical reality (e.g., year outside 2019-2020 or negative payment amounts), ensuring the Gold layer remains a "single source of truth."

3. Solving the "Big Data" bottleneck (Performance)
At this scale, simple queries were taking forever because the database was performing "full table scans" for every report.

Fix: To make the Gold layer usable, I implemented B-Tree indexes on the pickup_time and key join columns. This shifted the database from "reading everything" to "finding exactly what's needed," cutting down report generation time from minutes to seconds.
